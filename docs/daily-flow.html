How a day runs on AthleteIQ

Everything a coach, an athlete, and a parent actually do — mapped to the moments they do it in. One training day, front to back, plus the weekly rhythm around it.

Before Day One

One-time setup · ~10 minutes
1

Academy owner creates the account

Sign up on the Academy Portal with an email and password. A 14-day free trial starts automatically — full access, no card needed.

2

Coach signs in

Pick Coach and enter the coach password. admin123 sees every sport; a single-sport coach uses skatingcoach123, cricketcoach123, and so on.

3

Coach adds the squad

From the dashboard, Add Athletes — name, sport, and optional age. Add the whole roster in one sitting.

4

Athletes get their logins

Each athlete's password is generated from their name: first name + last initial + 123. So Jinesh Nanal logs in as jineshn123. Share it once; they're set.

A Training Day

Morning check-in → session → evening review
7:30 AM · Morning
Athlete wakes up, before anything else
Athlete

Daily Check-in

/checkin

Four quick sliders — Energy, Sleep, Soreness, Mood (1–10) — plus an optional note for anything niggling. Takes under 30 seconds.

  • The four scores combine into a Readiness score out of 100.
  • On submit, an AI coach message replies with a one-line nudge for the day.
  • Today-vs-yesterday comparison shows if they're trending up or down.
Athlete

Vitals Scan — optional

/vitals

Hold a fingertip to the phone camera for a 20-second heart-rate reading (camera-based rPPG). A resting-HR trend that adds an objective signal on top of the self-reported sliders.

Any time before · Pre-session
Coach checks the squad from their phone — no need to be at the ground
Coach

Read the squad board

/dashboard

The whole squad, grouped by sport. Each athlete card surfaces the signals that decide who trains hard today and who's managed:

  • Readiness from this morning's check-in.
  • AI Injury Risk score (the XGBoost model) with a red / yellow / green level.
  • ACWR (acute:chronic workload ratio) and sessions logged in the last 28 days.
  • A deception flag if a check-in looks inconsistent with the athlete's own pattern.
Green · train as planned Yellow · monitor / modify Red · pull back load
Coach

Generate today's session plan

/session-planner

Tell it the match context — no match, match in 3+ days, tomorrow, or today — and the AI builds a drill plan that respects the squad's current readiness and workload, so you're not overloading tired legs before a game.

5:00 – 7:00 PM · Session
Training happens — coach logs as it runs or right after
Coach

Mark attendance

/dashboard

One tap per athlete — present or absent — straight from the squad board.

Coach

Log the session load

Log Session

For each athlete, pick how hard they went: Light, Medium, or Heavy (auto-mapped to RPE 4 / 6 / 8), or mark sat out. The whole squad in a minute.

  • This feeds ACWR and recomputes each athlete's injury risk for tomorrow's board.
  • If a session goes unlogged, the dashboard shows an amber → urgent reminder so the data doesn't drift.
Evening · Optional
Wind-down from home — done when useful, not required every day
Athlete

Review the personal dashboard

/athlete-dashboard

Readiness and wellness trend charts, personal AI insights, and their own injury-risk read — so the athlete owns their recovery, not just the coach.

Athlete

Wind down with Meditation

/meditation

Short guided mental-performance sessions for recovery and focus before sleep.

Parent

Check the recovery portal

/parent

Parent signs in with the child's name and sees a plain-language recovery summary: how the week is trending, any active injuries, and a message from the coach — no jargon, no data overload.

When Something Happens

As-needed · an injury or a flag
Coach

Log an injury

/athlete/:name

Open the athlete's profile and record it — body part, type, severity, and notes. The injury model factors it into risk scoring, and the parent portal automatically reflects the recovery status.

The Weekly Rhythm

End of week · coach → parent
Coach

Send the weekly report

/athlete/:name → Weekly Report

Generate a per-athlete PDF report — the week's readiness, workload, and flags — add a personal coach note, and send it to the parent over WhatsApp in one tap.

  • Parents get a professional weekly touchpoint without you writing a message from scratch.
  • The same report lives in the athlete's history for the season record.
The loop

Morning check-ins and session logs feed the readiness and injury models → the models flag who to manage → the coach adjusts load and messages parents → healthier, better-informed athletes. Every day the data gets sharper, and the flags get more trustworthy.
